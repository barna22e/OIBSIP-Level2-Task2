Lata Mangeshkar

Indian playback singer and music director

1929-2022

Lata Mangeshkar is an Indian playback singer and music director who is regarded as one of the greatest singers in the history of Indian cinema. She was born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, India, into a musical family. Her father, Dinanath Mangeshkar, was a renowned classical singer and theater actor.


Lata Mangeshkar began her career as a playback singer in 1942 with the Marathi film "Kiti Hasaal." She gained widespread recognition and fame with her song "Aayega Aanewala" from the movie "Mahal" in 1949. Since then, she has sung for over a thousand films in various Indian languages, including Hindi, Marathi, Bengali, Tamil, and Telugu.
Lata Mangeshkar's voice is known for its versatility, range, and clarity. She has sung a wide range of songs, including classical, devotional, romantic, and patriotic songs, and has collaborated with some of the greatest composers and lyricists in Indian music history.
Lata Mangeshkar has won numerous awards and honors throughout her career, including the Padma Bhushan, Padma Vibhushan, and Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian award in India. She has also been awarded the Dadasaheb Phalke Award, the highest honor in Indian cinema.
Apart from singing, Lata Mangeshkar has also acted in a few films, produced a Marathi film, and composed music for a few movies. She has also been actively involved in philanthropic work, including supporting charitable organizations and donating to various causes.
Lata Mangeshkar's contribution to Indian music has been immense, and she continues to be an inspiration to aspiring singers and musicians. Her voice and music have touched the hearts of millions of people around the world, and she is considered a cultural icon and a living legend in India.
